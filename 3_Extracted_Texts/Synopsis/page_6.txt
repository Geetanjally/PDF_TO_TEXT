This survey reviews key platforms that convert scanned PDFs to PowerPoint using Optical
Character Recognition OCR.
1. Adobe Acrobat
e Description: An industry-leading tool that uses robust OCR to convert scanned PDFs.
e Pros: High-quality conversion, excellent preservation of formatting.
e Cons: Requires a paid subscription for full features.
e Link: Adobe Acrobat PDF to PPT Converter
2. Smallpdf
e Description: An online, user-friendly platform with a combined PDF to PPT and OCR
tool.
e Pros: Simple, all-in-one workflow; emphasizes security.
e Cons: OCR is a paid Pro feature.
e  Link: Smallpdf PDF to PPT Converter
3. Nitro
e Description: A free online tool that uses OCR to convert scanned PDFs to PPT.
e Pros: Free to use, no registration needed, browser-based.
e Cons: Paid desktop version Nitro Pro is required for advanced features like batch
processing.
e Link: Nitro PDF to PPT Converter
2.2 Limitations of Existing Systems
e Focus only on basic text extraction rather than contextual refinement.
e Struggle with noisy classroom notes, mixed diagrams, and inconsistent handwriting.
e Limited or no support for generating study-ready formats such as bullet points,
summaries, or slides.
